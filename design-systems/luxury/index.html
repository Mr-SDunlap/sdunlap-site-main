Heritage · Essay

What we mean when we say bespoke

by Élise Marchand

The word has been borrowed by everyone, so we should say precisely what we mean by it. A bespoke garment begins as a conversation, becomes a paper pattern that exists for you alone, and ends — a season later — as something no one else will ever own.

“Sixty hours of handwork,
none of it visible, all of it felt.”

The pattern is archived in the maison, in your name. 1 Return in ten years and we will cut for you again, adjusting for the decade — kindly, and without comment.

1. The archive presently holds 4,112 patterns, the oldest from 1989.
